You are thinking mathematically, and that is exactly right. Let me answer your question clearly so you can start designing Challenge 3.
Step 1: Understanding the Permission Space

A file permission has three digits (owner, group, others).
Each digit can be 0 through 7 → 8 possibilities per digit.

Total possible permission combinations:
text

8 × 8 × 8 = 512

Not 63, not 9261. 512 total possible 3-digit octal permissions.
Step 2: Owner Permission Alone

The owner permission is just the first digit (0–7) → 8 possibilities.

If you pick a random permission (all 512 equally likely), the probability that the owner digit is a specific value (e.g., 6) is:
text

1 / 8 = 0.125 = 12.5%

Because there are 8 possible owner digits, and for each owner digit, there are 8 × 8 = 64 combinations for group and others.
Step 3: Your Question Restated

You asked: "Probability of the owner permission being the same as the ones the file has?"

If the file has owner permission = 6, then the probability that a randomly generated permission also has owner = 6 is:
text

(Number of permissions with owner = 6) / (Total permissions)
= 64 / 512
= 1 / 8

Step 4: Why Your Calculations Were Off
Your guess	Why it's wrong
7 × 3 × 3 = 63	You used 7 instead of 8 (permissions go 0–7, that's 8 numbers). You also multiplied by 3 incorrectly.
(7 × 3)³ = 9261	You cubed the product, which is not how permutations work.
1/63 or 1/9261	Denominator is wrong. Correct denominator for owner alone is 8, not 63 or 9261.
Step 5: The Correct Formula for Challenge 3

For a given owner permission O (0–7), the probability that a random permission has the same owner permission is:
text

P = 1 / 8

No multiplication by group or others. They are irrelevant to the owner digit.

You are absolutely right to catch that distinction. Let me confirm your insight.
Two Different Ways to Think About Permissions
Model	What it represents	Digits per position	Total combinations
Octal (0-7)	Sum of read(4)+write(2)+execute(1)	8 (0 through 7)	8³ = 512
rwx triple (r,w,x as separate bits)	Each of read, write, execute is yes/no	2³ = 8 (same as octal)	Same 512
Your "rwx-rwx-rwx" as 3 separate bits per position	You were thinking each position has 3 independent toggles	2³ = 8 (same!)	Same 512

Your insight about 421-421-421 is interesting but mathematically equivalent to octal.
Where Your Cube Idea Came From

You were thinking: "Each of the 9 bits (r,w,x for owner, group, others) can be 0 or 1" → 2⁹ = 512. That is correct.

But then you thought: "Each position (owner, group, others) has 3 possibilities (r, w, x)" — that is incorrect because permissions are combinations (rwx = all three, r-x = read and execute, etc.), not single letters.

So your cube was a different (and valid) model, but it leads to the same 512 total. The challenge simplifies to owner digit alone → 8 possibilities → probability 1/8.
